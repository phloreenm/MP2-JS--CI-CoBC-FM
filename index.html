Greek colonies in Dobrudja

Three main ancient greek citadels on western shores of the Black Sea Histria

Between the 8th and 6th centuries BC, a phenomenon occurred that historians refer to as the great Greek colonisation. Colonists headed out from the great centres of ancient Greek civilisation in all directions, founding urban centres that kept close commercial, cultural and political ties with the cities of origin. The Black Sea area, in fact, was surrounded on all sides by Greek colonies.

On the western shores of the Black Sea, on what is now Romania, the Greek colonists from Miletus, on the eastern shore of the Ionian Sea, founded the cities of Histria, also known as Istros, and Tomis, while the colonists from Heraclea Pontica, on the southern shore of the Black Sea, 100 km east of the Bosphorus, founded the city of Callatis.
